# Student Physical Activity, Stress Coping, and Academic Performance  

## Overview  
This research project explores the relationship between **students' physical activity levels**, their **confidence in coping with stress**, and their **academic performance**. The goal is to determine whether regular exercise contributes to better stress management and academic success.  

## Research Question  
**What is the relationship between students' physical activity levels, their confidence in coping with stress, and their academic performance?**  

## Data Collection  
- The data was collected via surveys given to **incoming freshmen** before their arrival at **Truman State University**.  
- A similar survey was distributed to **current juniors**.  
- The data from both groups was combined into a single dataset, with **matched-pairs analyzed separately**.  

## Dataset  
Key variables in the dataset include:  
- **`Physical_Activity_Stress`**: A 1-4 scale measuring how much students use physical activity as a stress-coping mechanism.  
- **`Stress_Coping`**: Self-reported confidence in managing stress.  
- **`Exercise_150_Min`**: Binary variable indicating if a student exercises at least 150 minutes per week.  
- **`Study_Skills`**: Confidence in study skills, serving as an academic performance indicator.  
- **`Intellectual_Score`**: Composite measure approximating academic performance.  

## Analysis & Methodology  
- **Descriptive Statistics**: Summarizing key distributions and data trends.  
- **Data Visualization**:  
  - **Histograms & Boxplots**: Exploring distributions of physical activity and stress-coping confidence.  
  - **Scatter Plots & Regression Models**: Identifying relationships between physical activity and academic confidence.  
  - **Correlation Heatmaps**: Examining connections among physical activity, stress coping, and academic performance.  
- **Machine Learning Models**:  
  - Data preprocessing includes **feature scaling** and **handling missing values**.  
  - **Linear Regression & Random Forest Regression** models predict academic performance based on physical activity and stress-coping confidence.  

## Technologies & Libraries Used  
- **Python** (for data analysis and visualization)  
- **pandas** – Data manipulation  
- **seaborn & matplotlib** – Data visualization  
- **scikit-learn** – Machine learning models  

## Key Findings (Preliminary)  
- Students who engage in **regular physical activity** tend to have **higher confidence in coping with stress**.  
- There is a **positive correlation** between **exercise and study skills confidence**, suggesting that students who exercise more may feel **more academically prepared**.  
- The correlation between **stress-coping confidence and academic performance** suggests that **mental well-being plays a role in student success**.
